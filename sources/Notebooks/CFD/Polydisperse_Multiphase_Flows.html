                              1. Introduction, NDF

                              Definitions

                              • Multiphase: Composited of various phases, specifically, 1 phase cont and other phases disperse
                              • Disperse: One or more phases are composed of clearly identifiable discrete entities (elem): particles (solid) / droplets (liquid) / bubbles (gas)
                              • Polydisperse: Properties of each entity can be different (<=> Monodisperse for same properties, e.g., same size particles …)

                              Industrial applications: spray, bubble tower, etc.

                              Coordinates

                              • External: spatial (physical space)

                                x≡(x1,x2,x3);dx≡dx1 dx2 dx3

                              • Internal: the properties of the dispersed entities (phase space)

                                ξ≡(ξ1,ξ2,…,ξm);dξ≡dξ1 dξ2…dξm

                                m internal coord of interest

                              Number Density Function (NDF)

                              Similar to probability density function (PDF) in statistics

                              nξ(t,x,ξ)

                              nξ dx dξ: expected # of entities in dx & dξ (to solve nξ => describe the dispersed entity distributions)

                              0th Order Moment

                              For a variable v in a PDF f, the kth order moment of f: ∫−∞∞vkf dv

                              • 0th order moment: ∫−∞∞f dv=1
                              • 1th order moment: ∫−∞∞vf dv => the expectation
                              • 2nd order moment: related to the variance

                              In NDF, the 0th order moment of m: Total number concentration

                              N(t,x)≡mξ,0≡∫Ωξnξ(ξ) dξ

                              Since nξ is the (expected) number unit physical space volume, with all possible ξ (cancel the dependence of ξ)

                              An arbitrary moment:

                              mξ,k≡∫Ωξξ1k1⋯ξmkm nξ(ξ) dξ

                              So different NDFs can be chosen: length, volume, mass (if the material is specified and known)

                              Length-Based NDF

                              Length base (ξ→L): nL(t,x,L) [1L31L]

                              nL(t,x,L) dL: expected # density b/w L & L+dL

                              • Total num conc (0th order):

                                N≡mL,0=∫0∞nL(L) dL[1L3]

                                Mean particle size (one def, the eigenlength)

                                L10≡1N∫0∞LnL(L) dL=mL,1mL,0[1/L31/L2=1L]

                              • kth order:

                                mL,k≡∫0∞LknL(L) dL

                                Mean particle size (one def): mL,k+1mL,k

                              • Sauter mean diameter: L32=mL,3mL,2

                              => can convert to higher dimension (volume density …)

                              Assume: V∼L3: V=kVL3 (k - vol shape factor)

                              Volume-density function:

                              VL(t,x,L) where VL≡kVL3nL

                              Volume-fraction density: (normalized VL)

                              αV(t,x,L) where αV≡L3nL∫0∞L3nL(L) dL(=VL∫LVL dL)

                              Volume-Based NDF

                              Volume-based NDF: nV(t,x,V)

                              nL(L) dL=nV(V) dV=dV=3kVL2 dLnV(kVL3) 3kVL2 dL

                              Convert between nV and nL:

                              nL(L)=3kVL2nV(kVL3)mV,k≡∫0∞VknV(V) dV=∫0∞(kVL3)knL(L) dL=kVk mL,3k

                              Mass-Based NDF

                              Mass-based NDF: nM(t,x,M)

                              Consider to connect with length: M=kMLDf (Df is the fractal dimension, 1<Df≤3, for spheric, Df=3)

                              nL(L)=DfkMLDf−1 nM(kMLDf)mM,k=kMk mL,Dfk ;mM,1=∫0∞MnM dM(total ptc mass)

                               

                              2. PBE for Fine Particles

                              Population Balance Equation (PBE)

                              The discretized section is the fine particles

                              The particles:

                              • Moving with the fluid (velocity = vf)
                              • Aggregation (coalescence) <– (Reversible) –> Breakage

                              Recall 1D NDF: n(t,x,V) [1L31L3]⇒[1Vphy1Vphase]

                              PBE:∂n∂t+∂vfn∂x=Γ∂2n∂x2+C

                              sim to general fluid equations (e.g., mass balance): Change related to time + Advection = Diffusion (+ Aggregation/Breakage)

                              (vf - fluid/particle velocity (known); diffusion part: Γ - diff coefficient; C - source term due to agg & bre)

                              where

                              C=12∫0Va(V−V′,V′)n(V−V′)n(V′) dV′−∫0∞a(V,V′)n(V)n(V′) dV′+∫V∞b(V′)N(V|V′)n(V′) dV′−b(V)n(V)[1L31L31T]

                              (a(V,V′) and b(V) - kernel functions (trends of aggregation/breakage, for agg, two particle volumes V and V′); N(V|V′) - daugher dist func)

                              [a]=VphysT

                              Moments

                              Total aggregate (particle) number density

                              m0(t,x)=∫0∞n(t,x,V) dV[1L3]

                              Disperse phase vol frac (dim-less)

                              m1(t,x)=∫0∞Vn(t,x,V) dV=αp[ ]

                              (n(t,x,V) dV - number in unit physical vol, dV phase space vol; Vn(t,x,V) dV - Vol of agg in unit physical vol, dV phase space vol)

                              Dimensionless partical size dist (PSD)

                              ψ(ξ)=n(t,x,V)m1(t,x)m02(t,x),ξ=Vm0m1∫0∞ψ(ξ) dξ=1,∫0∞ξψ(ξ) dξ=1

                              (ξ - dimensionless internal coordinate)

                              => ψ‘s 0th and 1st order moments are both 1

                              2nd order moment

                              m2(t,x)=∫0∞V2n(t,x,V) dV

                              Variance: m2−m12/m0

                              Characteristic scales

                              The particle flow characteristics ..

                              • Charateristic scales: Length L, velocity U, agg vol ζ, total agg # den Nt

                              • Disp-phase (char) vol frac: ϕp=ζNt

                              • Aggregation time scale: The original phase contains more particles and particles occupies more volume => agg and bre both more likely to happen in a denser system, the chance will be higher

                                τa=1a(ζ,ζ)Nt∝1Nt∝1ϕp

                              • Breakage time scale:

                                τb=1b(ζ)

                              • More (dim-less) characteristic scales:

                                ξ=Vζ,n∗=nζnt,x∗=xL,vf∗=vfU,t∗=tΓL2

                              • Dim-less PBE (for some point in space)

                                ∂n∗∂t∗+Pe⁡∂vf∗n∗∂x∗−∂2n∗∂x∗2=Daa⁡[12∫0ξa∗(ξ−ξ′,ξ′) n∗(ξ−ξ′) n∗(ξ′) dξ′−∫0∞a∗(ξ,ξ′) n∗(ξ) n∗(ξ′) dξ′]+Dab⁡[∫ξ∞b∗(ξ′) N(ξ|ξ′) n∗(ξ′) dξ′−b∗(ξ) n∗(ξ)]

                                where

                                a∗(ξ,ξ′)=a(V,V′)a(ζ,ζ′) ,b∗(ξ)=b(V)b(ζ)

                              The dimensionless numbers Pe and Da

                              • Peclet number (Pe): sim to Reynold number, advection / diffusion = diff char time / adv char time

                                Pe=ULΓ=L2/ΓL/U(Re=ULν)

                              • Damkohler number (Da)

                                • Aggregate Damkohler, the ability of agg / diff

                                  Daa=a(ζ,ζ)L2NtΓ=L2/Γ1/(a(ζ,ζ)Nt)

                                • Breakage Damkohler, the ability of bre / diff

                                  Dab=b(ζ)L2Γ=1/Γ1/b(ζ)

                              Major Operating Regimes

                              High ϕp and Nt means dense fluid and high fraction of particles, agg/bre take place more often. Otherwise, dilute

                              Diffusion: average the particles spatially, leads to homog. dist.; Aggregation/Breakage: make changes locally, tends to make difference in space

                              • Dilute: ϕp<<1 (10−3); Daa,Dab<<1 (10−2): Spatially homogeneous / well-mixed, the major effect is diffusion

                                Volume-average PBE (a simplification):

                                ∂n^∂t=12∫0Va^(V−V′,V′) n^(V−V′) n^(V′) dV′−∫0∞a^(V,V′) n^(V) n^(V′) dV′+∫V∞b^(V′) N(V|V′) n^(V′) dV′−b^(V) n^(V)

                                (a^,b^ - volume-average a and b kernels)

                              • Moderately dense: Daa,Dab< (cannot neglect any section)

                                Full equation (PBE)

                              • Dense: Daa,Dab>102: local equilibrium / steady state

                                C=0⇒neq

                               

                              3. KE for Gas-Particle Flow

                              Velocity-based NDF

                              for particle velocity that cannot be regarded as vf as the fluid flow velocity (not move with the fluid flows), U as a random var.

                              nU(t,x,v), v=(v1,v2,v3)

                              • Total num conc (0th order moment)

                                N=mU,0≡∫ΩVnU(v) dv

                              • Average particle velocity (1st order moment)

                                Up≡1N∫Ωvv nU(v) dv=mU,1mU,0

                              Kenetic Equation (KE)

                              Assumptions

                              • Monodisperse: all properties, except velocity, are the same
                              • Isothermal: neglect heat transfer
                              • Fluid drag, gravity and buoyancy forces

                              Moments

                              The normalized NDF n(t,x,v):

                              • 0th order moment: (αp - the disp-phase vol frac) normalized by times the vol of 1 particle

                                αp≡∫−∞∞n dvvol frac

                              • 1st order moment: (Up - mean velocity)

                                αpUp≡∫−∞∞vn dv

                              • 2nd order moment: (Θp - granular temperature, [v2] ~ energy)

                                αpΘp+αpUp2≡∫−∞∞v2n dv

                              The Kenetic Equation

                              ∂n∂t+∂vn∂x+∂An∂v=C

                              • The part of ∂An/∂v is actually the transfer in phase space (A for acceleration: buoyancy (pressure) + gravitation + drag froce)

                                A≡−1ρp∂pg∂x+gx+1τp(Ug−v)

                              • C is the dispersed section, which should be related to some sudden-change force: collision

                                C≡1τc(neq−n)

                                (ρp - partial density; τp,τc - drag/collision char time scale; neq - for equilibrium distribution)

                              Need to also consider the cont. fluid equation => coupled

                              Mass Balance ∂αg∂t+∂(αgUg)∂x=0Momentum Balance (gas) ∂(αgUg)∂t+∂(αgUgUg)∂x=αgνg∂2Ug∂x2−αgρg∂pg∂x+αggx+ρpαpρgτp(Up−Ug)αp+αg=1

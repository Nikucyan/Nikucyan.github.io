          Laplace Transform

          A Laplace transform: (by def) change the var from t to s

          f(t), t>0.F(s)=L(f(t))=∫0∞e−stf(t) dt=limτ→∞∫0τe−stf(t) dt(if convergese)

            • So: L(1)=1/s,(s>0)

          Inverse Laplace Transform

          Inverse L: L(f(t))=F(s)⟷f(t)=L−1(F(s))

          Theorem: Distinct continuous functions on [0,∞) have distinct L

          Linearity of L−1: L−1(aF(s)+bG(s))=af(t)+bg(t)

          • ex: Heaveside function ua(t)={1t≥a00≤t<a (step func, only need to consider the non-zero part in Laplace transform)

            L(ua(t))=∫0∞e−stua(t) dt=∫a∞e−st dt=e−st−s|a∞=e−sas(s>0)
            • L−1(e−sa/s)=ua(t)
